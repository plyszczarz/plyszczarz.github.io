LEM Wrocław Motorsport







About LEM Wrocław Motorsport

LEM Wrocław Motorsport is a science club at the University of Science and Technology in Wrocław, Poland, dedicated to pioneering innovative electric motorcycles and various other engineering projects. With a strong commitment to technological advancement and hands-on experience, the club offers students a platform to hone practical skills in electronics, software, and mechanical engineering. LEM Wrocław Motorsport actively participates in numerous international competitions, such as MotoStudent and Moto Engineering Italy, showcasing their cutting-edge projects and gaining valuable experience on a global stage.








My Role

As an electronic team member, I was involved in various tasks including soldering, connecting batteries, and managing the Battery Management System (BMS) for our motorcycles. I also created electric harnesses and developed software to integrate sensors like GPS into our motorbikes. My role was crucial in ensuring the reliability and performance of our electric motorcycles.








LEM Tachyon

LEM Tachyon, is an electric motorcycle designed for Moto3, built for the MotoStudent competition in Spain and Moto Engineering 2024. In this project, I was responsible for managing the Battery Management System (BMS), overseeing battery assembly, and integrating the GPS system into the bike.

LEM SSV

One of four-wheeled projects is the LEM SSV, an off-road vehicle capable of reaching speeds of 110 km/h. In this project, I contributed to the development of the electrical harness for the entire vehicle.

LEM Photon

LEM Photon is an electric motorcycle designed for Moto3, built specifically for the MotoStudent competition in Spain. It boasts a maximum speed of 197 km/h. In this project, I assembled the battery, programmed and mounted sensors such as GPS and temperature sensors, and managed the Battery Management System.

LEM Thunder

Lem Thunder is the most advanced off-road enduro electric motorcycle in the country, it has competed in enduro events. I was involved in the development of the electrical harness for this vehicle.
